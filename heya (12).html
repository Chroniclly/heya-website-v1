🎯 Our Objective

Our Objective

HEYA is building a movement of young people to ensure housing affordability and combat the homelessness crisis. Through financial literacy education, political lobbying, and outreach, we will make housing more attainable for youth. Together, we will secure legislators and legislative changes that prioritize youth housing needs and empower youth to fight for their future. Are you ready to join us?

⚠️ The Problem

Why This Matters

It's well known that the housing market in the United States is more competitive than ever, with 70% of young adults in America saying that it's more difficult to buy a home than it was in their parents' generation. And this consensus isn't just a feeling; home prices have skyrocketed 197% since 2000, and the average price of a family home is 5 times a family's average income.

Source: covenanthouse.org ↗ Source: livenowfox.com ↗

These high prices are particularly troubling for youth entering the housing market for the first time. Purchasing property has become so difficult that the average age of first-time buyers has risen from 30 to 40, and 62% of Gen Z state that they dont believe they will ever be able to afford a home.

Source: nar.realtor ↗ Source: medium.com ↗

Here at HEYA, we believe that this needs to change. Affordable housing should be attainable for everyone. We aim to support better policies and distribute education on housing to help youth in America reach financial stability and purchase property, improving the health of our communities and our country.

🎯 Our Focus Areas

Tackling the Issues That Matter Most

🏗️

Incentivization of Housing Production

As housing supply increases, housing affordability will inadvertently increase. This will increase youth accessibility to housing.

📋

Increased Regulation over the Renting Industry

To increase housing attainability, rent must be lowered so that future families and individuals can continue to afford shelter ("WATCH: Senate").

🎓

Mandatory Financial Literacy Education

To ensure post-purchase success, young individuals should be educated about the associated expenses of homebuying, such as debt, home services, and the impact on personal credit.
